Burger

Description

This homemade burger recipe is all about simple, flavorful ingredients and easy steps. By seasoning ground beef with salt, pepper, and optional garlic powder, you can create juicy patties that cook up perfectly on a grill or in a skillet. As the patties cook, cheese melts over them, adding richness. The buns are toasted just enough to be warm and crisp, ready to hold all the fresh toppings like lettuce, tomato, and pickles. With your favorite condiments, this burger offers a classic, satisfying meal that's quick to prepare and always delicious.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef (80% lean)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Black pepper (to taste)
  • Garlic powder (optional, to taste)
  • 4 burger buns
  • 4 slices of cheese (optional, cheddar or American)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Tomato slices
  • Pickles (optional)
  • Ketchup, mustard, or mayo (optional, for toppings)

steps

  1. Season the ground beef with salt, pepper, and garlic powder (if using).
  2. Shape the seasoned beef into four patties, making a small indentation in the center of each.
  3. Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Cook the patties for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until they reach your desired doneness.
  5. Toast the burger buns lightly on the grill or in a toaster.
  6. Place a slice of cheese on each patty during the last minute of cooking, allowing it to melt.
  7. Assemble the burger by placing the cooked patty on the bottom half of each bun.
  8. Top with lettuce, tomato slices, pickles, and your choice of condiments.
  9. Place the top half of the bun on the assembled burger, then serve and enjoy!
